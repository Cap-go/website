# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Capgo speaks to Capacitor developers, mobile engineering leads, founders, and CTOs who need to ship production mobile updates without waiting on app store review cycles. They arrive with practical pressure: a hotfix is needed, a release process is too slow, an incumbent tool is risky or expensive, or they need proof that OTA updates, native builds, channels, rollback, and monitoring are safe enough for real users.

## Product Purpose

The website exists to make Capgo the obvious production platform for Capacitor release workflows. It should convert skeptical technical buyers by showing that teams can ship live updates, automate native builds, recover from bad releases, and monitor delivery with less release friction and more control. Success means visitors understand the workflow quickly, trust the operational proof, and move toward registration, docs, pricing, or a sales conversation.

## Brand Personality

Fast, technical, independent. The voice is direct and practical, with enough confidence to challenge slow incumbent release workflows. It should feel like a capable engineering team showing the working system, not a generic SaaS brand polishing vague benefits.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS pages with interchangeable feature-card grids, decorative gradient text, soft beige editorial styling, fake enterprise gloss, and abstract promises that are not backed by workflow proof. Avoid overplaying fear around shutdowns or review delays without a concrete migration path. Avoid designs that feel like a copied competitor template, a cyberpunk dashboard costume, or a consumer-app onboarding page.

## Design Principles

1. Proof before claims: show real workflows, command examples, metrics, customer evidence, and operational details before asking for trust.
2. Developer workflow first: pages should map to how builders actually ship, debug, rollback, price, and integrate Capgo.
3. Speed with control: every high-velocity claim should pair with guardrails like channels, rollback, compatibility, monitoring, and release visibility.
4. Independent and transparent: Capgo's bootstrapped, open, long-term posture is a differentiator and should read as durable, not scrappy.
5. Reduce decision friction: keep copy compact, make next actions obvious, and route readers to the exact docs, pricing, demo, or signup path they need.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA across marketing pages. Keep body text contrast at or above 4.5:1, preserve visible keyboard focus, support reduced motion, do not hide content behind animation triggers, and keep responsive headings from overflowing on mobile. Localized pages should keep the same clarity and control as English source content.
